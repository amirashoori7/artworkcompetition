Competition History


The Math-Art competition is an innovative, practical project that resulted from the need to promote creativity in problem solving by encouraging learners to explore the real-life links between mathematics and the arts (which is one of the main aims of the STEAM concept in education). The Govan Mbeki Mathematics Development Centre (GMMDC) based at the Nelson Mandela University adopted the STEAM concept in 2017 as one of the important educational drivers of the future.

The GMMDC launched the MathArt competition project in high schools of the Eastern Cape Province in March 2018. The MathArt competition was piloted with the support of the Nelson Mandela University's School of Music, Art and Design (SoMAD) and the Department of Basic Education in the Eastern Cape Province. Entrants were required to come up with their own concept of MathArt, within a suggested project theme. Each entry had to include a 300-word description of how their artwork linked mathematics and art. There were two age categories in 2018: Grades 8 & 9 and Grades 10 to 12. Judging was mostly based on the creativity in linking mathematics and art.

The regional pilot competition in 2018 was overwhelmingly successful. It attracted 113 entries, mostly from learners in 36 previously-disadvantaged schools in the Eastern Cape. Most of the entries were exhibited at the Nelson Mandela Metropolitan Art Museum in Port Elizabeth in May that year.

The pilot South African MathArt project has attracted the attention of the international math-art community. The Experience Workshop Global STEAM Network (www.experienceworkshop.org), which have been organizing International Children and Youth Math-Art Exhibits since 2012, have initiated the international collaboration and supported the competition from the beginning. Since 2018, the artworks of the South African children were exhibited at the "John Hiigli Children and Youth Math-Art" exhibits, organized by the Experience Workshop at the Bridges conferences, the world's largest math-art community's annual meetings at various locations around the globe. Thirty selected MathArt entries from South Africa were part of this exhibit at the annual International Bridges Conference, which took place at the National Museum of Science and Technology in Stockholm, Sweden, in July 2018.

The MathArt project was expanded, with the support of Umalusi and the Department of Basic Education, to a national school competition in 2019. A total of 593 competition entries were received from 75 schools in the 8 provinces in South Africa in this year. All the entries were again exhibited at the Nelson Mandela Metropolitan Art Museum in Port Elizabeth in June 2019. Once again, a selection of the best South African entries were invited by Experience Workshop organizers to be exhibited at the 2019 International Bridges Conference in July, this time in Linz, Austria.

Subsequently, more provincial and national education stakeholders in South Africa have expressed a keen interest to join this national project as it seeks to promote STEAM education with the emphasis on integrating innovation and creativity as vital skills to meet the challenges of the 4IR.

The collaboration between the GMMDC and the Experience Workshop Global STEAM Network have been fruitful and will continue to grow as the national MathArt school competition programme expands every year.
